# 《逆骨问仙》Style Guide

## Tone

Write in Chinese with grounded pastoral texture, clear serial momentum, and gradually widening xianxia wonder.

Prefer:

- Real details: dry soil, water jars, herb leaves, old channels, family meals, village arguments.
- Quick visible consequences.
- Fang Chen acting, testing, noticing, deciding.
- Warmth without softness; mystery without stalling.
- Simple but evocative prose.

Avoid:

- 苦大仇深 openings, massacres, public humiliation revenge, or oppressive tragedy as the main engine.
- Slow scenic wandering without payoff.
- Explaining "修仙界" before the characters see consequences.
- Overly ornate description that delays action.
- Long repeated inner monologue about uncertainty.

## Chapter Rhythm

Default chapter length: 2200-3200 Chinese characters.

Each chapter must include at least three forward movements:

1. A real pressure: drought, water rationing, sick villager, dying herbs, family worry, village dispute, mountain danger.
2. A gain or clue: Taixu Seed dew, changed herb, root direction, dream phrase, sharper perception, hidden water sign.
3. A consequence or hook: exposure risk, roots moving, well water dropping, someone noticing, mountain sound, new object, new danger.

Do not spend a full chapter only observing. If a scene is quiet, make the result sharp.

## 爽点 Design

Early爽点 should be practical and immediate:

- A dying herb recovers.
- A medicine works when it should have failed.
- Fang Chen finds water by noticing what others missed.
- Lin Qinghe confirms a hidden pattern through herb knowledge.
- Villagers dismiss superstition, but Fang Chen's method produces results.
- A small secret advantage saves someone or prevents loss.

Every 1-3 chapters in the opening arc should deliver a visible payoff, not just setup.

## Opening Five-Chapter Mandate

Chapter 1:

- Drought, channel repair, gray seed, impossible dew.

Chapter 2:

- Lin Qinghe appears through an urgent herb or medicine problem.
- Dew creates a visible herb effect.
- The benefit creates a dangerous reaction.

Chapter 3:

- Water conflict or old well crisis.
- Fang Chen investigates roots actively.
- Taixu Seed responds more strongly.

Chapter 4:

- Use herb/dew/medicine to save someone or solve a concrete village crisis.
- Let at least one outsider or villager notice something odd.

Chapter 5:

- Open the first threshold toward the hidden cultivation world through mountain, spring, talisman, wounded stranger, or old inscription.

## Viewpoint Knowledge

At the start:

- Fang Chen does not know cultivation exists.
- Lin Qinghe does not know cultivation exists.
- Villagers do not know cultivation exists.

Narration must not casually use cultivation terminology as fact before characters have a reason to name it. Use sensory consequences first: warmth, breath, dew, root light, water pull, unusual vitality.

## Lin Qinghe Usage

Use Lin Qinghe as an active co-investigator:

- She diagnoses herbs.
- She challenges Fang Chen's assumptions.
- She helps hide or test strange effects.
- She makes practical choices under pressure.
- She can notice risks Fang Chen misses.

Do not write her as a helpless witness to Fang Chen's miracle.

## Taixu Seed Rules

- Small effects first.
- Practical use before grand power.
- Every benefit creates a trace.
- Repeated use increases attention from roots, water, mountain, hidden beings, or future cultivators.
- Fang Chen must earn understanding through experiments, failures, and consequences.

## Continuity Checklist

Before finalizing a chapter or outline, check:

- Did the chapter move the plot at least three times?
- Did Fang Chen solve something through observation or action?
- Did Lin Qinghe, if present, keep agency?
- Did the Taixu Seed help without becoming free omnipotence?
- Did the village remain unaware of cultivation unless the chapter intentionally changes that?
- Is there a concrete ending hook?
- Did new canon require tracker notes?

## Useful Chapter-End Hook Types

- The old well drops overnight.
- Gray-white roots move toward a house, field, or herb plot.
- The Taixu Seed produces a new dream phrase.
- Lin Qinghe records a herb change that should be impossible.
- A villager notices Fang Chen's suspicious timing.
- A hidden spring, talisman, footprint, or wounded stranger appears.
- A small victory exposes a larger village-level danger.
